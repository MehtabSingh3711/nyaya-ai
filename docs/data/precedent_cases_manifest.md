# Indian Commercial & Contract Law: 100 Landmark Precedents Manifest (Corrected)

This manifest contains 100 landmark judgments of the Supreme Court of India, Privy Council, and High Courts that govern commercial transactions, contract enforcement, MSME overrides, privacy, and dispute resolution in India. These cases form the core of the `nyaya_precedents` database.

## 📂 Category 1: Offer, Acceptance, and Invitation to Treat (Cases 1–15)

| # | Case Name & Citation | Sub-Category | Key Legal Issue | Core Judicial Holding (Precedent Ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Lalman Shukla v. Gauri Datt** (1913) 11 ALJ 489 | Offer Communication | Can an offer be accepted without knowledge of it? | **No.** An offer must be communicated. Performing conditions of an offer without prior knowledge of it does not constitute a valid contract. |
| 2 | **Carlill v. Carbolic Smoke Ball Co.** [1893] 1 QB 256 | General Offer | Can an advertisement serve as a binding offer to the public? | **Yes.** General offers can be made to the public. Performance of the conditions in the offer serves as acceptance; notification of acceptance is not required. |
| 3 | **Bhagwandas Goverdhandas Kedia v. Girdharilal Parshottamdas & Co.** AIR 1966 SC 543 | Acceptance via Telex/Phone | Where and when is a contract complete under instantaneous communication? | A contract is completed only when acceptance is **heard/received by the offeror**, and the contract is formed at the place where acceptance is heard. |
| 4 | **Harvey v. Facey** [1893] AC 552 | Invitation to Treat | Does a statement of lowest price constitute an offer to sell? | **No.** Providing the lowest price of property is merely responding to an inquiry (invitation to treat), not an offer to sell. |
| 5 | **Balfour v. Balfour** [1919] 2 KB 571 | Intent to Contract | Are domestic agreements between spouses legally binding contracts? | **No.** Agreements made in domestic contexts generally lack the necessary intention to create legal relations. |
| 6 | **Entores Ltd. v. Miles Far East Corporation** [1955] 2 QB 327 | Inbound Telex | Does the postal rule apply to instantaneous communications? | **No.** For telex and instantaneous messages, the contract is complete only when the acceptance is received by the offeror. |
| 7 | **Felthouse v. Bindley** (1862) 11 CB (NS) 869 | Silent Acceptance | Can silence or mental assent be treated as a valid acceptance? | **No.** Acceptance must be communicated explicitly. Silence cannot be prescribed as a mode of acceptance. |
| 8 | **MacPherson v. Appanna** AIR 1951 SC 184 | Counter-Offer | Does a counter-proposal keep the original offer open? | **No.** A counter-proposal rejects the original offer. A contract is only concluded if there is absolute and unqualified acceptance. |
| 9 | **U.P. State Electricity Board v. Goel Electric Stores** AIR 1977 All 407 | Auction Tenders | Is the submission of a tender a binding contract? | **No.** A tender submission is an offer. The contract is formed only when the receiving authority officially accepts the tender. |
| 10 | **Powell v. Lee** (1908) 99 LT 284 | Unauthorized Acceptance | Is communication of acceptance by an unauthorized third party binding? | **No.** Communication of acceptance must be made by the offeree or their authorized agent; unauthorized communication is invalid. |
| 11 | **Hyde v. Wrench** (1840) 3 Beav 334 | Rejecting original offer | Does making a counter-offer destroy the original offer? | **Yes.** A counter-offer cancels the original offer, which cannot subsequently be accepted unless renewed by the offeror. |
| 12 | **Fisher v. Bell** [1961] 1 QB 394 | Price Display | Is the display of an item with a price tag in a shop window an offer? | **No.** It is an invitation to treat. The customer makes the offer when presenting the item to the cashier. |
| 13 | **Pharmaceutical Society of Great Britain v. Boots Cash Chemists** [1953] 1 QB 401 | Self-Service Displays | When is a sale concluded in a self-service retail shop? | The contract is concluded at the cashier counter when the cashier accepts the customer's offer to buy. |
| 14 | **Harris v. Nickerson** (1873) LR 8 QB 286 | Auction Cancellation | Does advertising an auction constitute an offer to hold it? | **No.** An advertisement of an auction is a declaration of intent, not an offer. A bidder cannot sue for travel expenses if the auction is cancelled. |
| 15 | **Dunlop v. Higgins** (1848) 1 HLC 381 | Postal Rule | Is acceptance complete when the letter is posted, even if delayed? | **Yes.** Once a letter of acceptance is posted, the acceptance is complete as against the offeror. |

## 💰 Category 2: Consideration & Privity of Contract (Cases 16–25)

| # | Case Name & Citation | Sub-Category | Key Legal Issue | Core Judicial Holding (Precedent Ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 16 | **Chinnaya v. Ramayya** (1882) ILR 4 Mad 137 | Moveable Consideration | Can consideration move from a third party to the contract? | **Yes.** Under Section 2(d) of the ICA, consideration may move from the promisee or "any other person" who is not a direct party. |
| 17 | **Tweddle v. Atkinson** [1861] EWHC QB J57 | Privity of Contract | Can a third-party beneficiary enforce a contract? | **No.** A person who is not a party to a contract cannot sue on it, even if the contract was made for their direct benefit. |
| 18 | **Dunlop Pneumatic Tyre Co. v. Selfridge & Co.** [1915] AC 847 | Price Maintenance | Can an agent enforce a contract on behalf of an undisclosed principal? | A stranger to the consideration cannot sue on a contract, upholding the core English rule of privity. |
| 19 | **Kedarnath Bhattacharji v. Gorie Mahomed** (1887) ILR 14 Cal 64 | Promissory Estoppel | Is a subscription promise for a public project binding? | **Yes.** If the promisee commences work on the faith of the subscription, the promise constitutes valid consideration. |
| 20 | **Dutton v. Poole** (1677) 2 Lev 210 | Family Exception | Can a child enforce a promise made by a parent to another sibling? | **Yes.** In family arrangements, close blood relations can form an exception to the strict rule of privity of consideration. |
| 21 | **M.C. Chacko v. State Bank of Travancore** AIR 1970 SC 504 | Trust Exception | Can a third party enforce a charge created on property for their benefit? | **Yes.** If a contract creates a trust or a specific charge on property in favor of a third party, they can enforce it. |
| 22 | **Chidambara Iyer v. P.S. Renga Iyer** AIR 1966 SC 193 *(corrected — was misnamed "P.S. Krishna Iyer" and misdated to 1920 Mad)* | Adequate Consideration | Can an illusory consideration support a binding contract? | Under Section 2(d), what qualifies as "valuable" consideration is a question the court determines; consideration must be real and of some value in the eyes of law but need not be financially adequate or equal. |
| 23 | **Durga Prasad v. Baldeo** (1880) ILR 3 All 221 | Desired Consideration | Is a promise made at the desire of a third party enforceable? | **No.** Consideration must be given **at the desire of the promisor**, not at the request of a third-party authority. |
| 24 | **Thomas v. Thomas** (1842) 2 QB 851 | Nominal Consideration | Is nominal rent (£1 per year) sufficient consideration? | **Yes.** Consideration must be sufficient but need not be adequate. Moral obligation alone is not consideration. |
| 25 | **Lampleigh v. Brathwait** (1615) Hob 105 | Past Service | Is past service rendered at the promisor's request valid consideration? | **Yes.** A past act done at the request of the promisor, followed by a subsequent promise of payment, is binding. |

## 🚫 Category 3: Capacity to Contract (Cases 26–33)

| # | Case Name & Citation | Sub-Category | Key Legal Issue | Core Judicial Holding (Precedent Ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 26 | **Mohori Bibee v. Dharmodas Ghose** (1903) 30 IA 114 | Minor's Agreement | What is the status of an agreement entered into by a minor? | **Void ab initio.** A contract entered into by a minor is completely void from the beginning and cannot be enforced. |
| 27 | **Khan Gul v. Lakha Singh** ILR (1928) 9 Lah 701 | Equitable Restitution | Can a minor be ordered to restore benefits obtained by fraud? | **Yes.** If a minor fraudulently misrepresents their age, equity requires them to restore the specific property/benefit. |
| 28 | **Leslie Ltd v. Sheill** [1914] 3 KB 607 | Restitution Limits | Can a minor be sued for money lent based on false age representation? | **No.** You cannot enforce a contract disguised as a tort action. Restitution stops when the money is spent/cannot be traced. |
| 29 | **Mohammad Syedol Ariffin v. Yeoh Ooi Gark** (1916) 2 AC 575 | Age Proof | How is a minor's age established in contract disputes? | S. 32 of the Evidence Act permits statements of deceased relatives (like entries in family records) to prove minority. |
| 30 | **Inder Singh v. Parmeshwardhari Singh** AIR 1957 Pat 491 | Unsound Mind | Is a contract entered into during a temporary lapse of sanity valid? | **No.** A person must be capable of understanding the contract and forming a rational judgment at the time of execution. |
| 31 | **Jai Indra Bahadur Singh v. Deputy Commissioner, Kheri** (1918) 45 IA 148 / AIR 1918 PC 40 | Lunatic's Necessaries | Can the estate of a person of unsound mind be charged for the supply of necessaries? | **Yes.** Under Section 68, a person who supplies necessaries (including funds to defend their estate in litigation) is entitled to be reimbursed from the incompetent person's property. |
| 32 | **Kunwarlal v. Surajmal** AIR 1963 MP 58 | Necessaries Scope | Does providing house rent to a minor for educational purposes constitute "necessaries" under Section 68? | **Yes.** Renting a house to a minor to live in and continue their studies qualifies as necessaries suited to their condition in life, and is recoverable from their estate. |
| 33 | **Sri Kakulam Subrahmanyam v. Kurra Subba Rao** (1948) 75 IA 115 / AIR 1948 PC 95 | Minor's Guardian | Can a natural guardian bind a minor to a contract for the sale/purchase of property? | **Yes.** A contract entered into by a natural guardian on behalf of a minor is binding and enforceable if it is for the **evident benefit or legal necessity** of the minor. |

## 🤝 Category 4: Free Consent — Coercion, Undue Influence, Fraud, Mistake (Cases 34–45)

| # | Case Name & Citation | Sub-Category | Key Legal Issue | Core Judicial Holding (Precedent Ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 34 | **Chikham Amiraju v. Chikham Seshamma** (1917) 41 Mad 33 | Coercion | Does a threat to commit suicide constitute coercion under S. 15? | **Yes.** A threat to commit suicide is a threat to commit an act forbidden by the Indian Penal Code, satisfying S. 15. |
| 35 | **Derry v. Peek** (1889) LR 14 App Cas 337 | Fraud | What constitutes fraud at common law? | Fraud is proved when a false representation has been made **knowingly, without belief in its truth**, or recklessly. |
| 36 | **Ladli Prasad v. Karnal Distillery Co.** AIR 1963 SC 1279 | Undue Influence | How is undue influence established in corporate transactions? | One party must hold a **dominant position** and use it to obtain an unfair advantage over the weaker party. |
| 37 | **Cundy v. Lindsay** (1878) 3 App Cas 459 | Mistake of Identity | Does a mistake as to the identity of a party make a contract void? | **Yes.** A mistake regarding identity prevents consensus ad idem, rendering the contract void ab initio. |
| 38 | **Phillips v. Brooks** [1919] 2 KB 243 | Face-to-Face Identity | Is a contract void if a swindler misrepresents identity face-to-face? | **No, it is voidable.** In face-to-face transactions, the seller intends to contract with the person in front of them. |
| 39 | **Allcard v. Skinner** (1887) 36 Ch D 145 | Spiritual Influence | Does a religious vow raise a presumption of undue influence? | **Yes.** A gift made to a spiritual adviser under religious influence raises a presumption of undue influence. |
| 40 | **Bisset v. Wilkinson** [1927] AC 177 | Opinion vs Fact | Is an erroneous statement of opinion fraud or misrepresentation? | **No.** An honest statement of opinion on a subject of which the maker has no special knowledge is not misrepresentation. |
| 41 | **Smith v. Hughes** (1871) LR 6 QB 597 | Unilateral Mistake | Does a buyer's mistaken belief about an item's quality invalidate a sale? | **No.** Caveat emptor applies. A unilateral mistake as to quality does not render the contract void. |
| 42 | **Bell v. Lever Brothers Ltd** [1932] AC 161 | Mutual Mistake | Does a shared mistake about an underlying contract fact void it? | Only if the mistake is mutual and relates to the **essential identity or existence** of the contract's subject matter. |
| 43 | **Raffles v. Wichelhaus** (1864) 2 H&C 906 | Latent Ambiguity | Is a contract binding if the parties mean two different ships of the same name? | **No.** Since there was a mutual misunderstanding of the subject matter, there was no consensus ad idem. |
| 44 | **Cooper v. Phibbs** (1867) LR 2 HL 149 | Mistake of Law/Private Right | Is a contract void if a party mistakenly rents their own property? | **Yes.** A mistake as to private rights or ownership is treated as a mistake of fact, rendering the lease void. |
| 45 | **Sherwood v. Walker** 66 Mich 568 (1887) | Mistake of Subject | Is a contract for a barren cow void if she turns out to be pregnant? | **Yes.** A mutual mistake about the essential nature of the animal (barren vs. breeding) goes to the root of the contract. |

## ⚖️ Category 5: Legality of Object & Public Policy (Cases 46–52)

| # | Case Name & Citation | Sub-Category | Key Legal Issue | Core Judicial Holding (Precedent Ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 46 | **Gherulal Parakh v. Mahadeodas Maiya** AIR 1959 SC 781 | Public Policy | Is a partnership to enter into wagering transactions void under S. 23? | **No.** A wagering contract is void but **not illegal**. A partnership to execute wagers is therefore not unlawful. |
| 47 | **Central Inland Water Transport Corp v. Brojo Nath Ganguly** AIR 1986 SC 1571 | Unconscionable Clauses | Can an employer arbitrarily terminate an employee with 3 months' notice? | **No.** Unconscionable, unfair, and oppressive terms in a standard-form contract between unequal parties violate public policy under S. 23. |
| 48 | **Pearce v. Brooks** (1866) LR 1 Ex 213 | Immoral Purpose | Can a coachbuilder recover hire charges for a brougham used for prostitution? | **No.** A contract to supply goods intended for an immoral purpose known to the seller is void for illegality. |
| 49 | **Ram Coomar Coondoo v. Chunder Canto Mookerjee** (1876) 4 IA 23 (PC) *(corrected — replaces fabricated "Nirmal Singh v. Wheeler, AIR 1967 All 262," which does not exist in any traceable record)* | Champerty | Are agreements to share litigation proceeds (champerty) illegal in India? | **No.** The rigid English rules of maintenance and champerty do not apply in India; a fair agreement to fund litigation for a share of the property recovered is not per se opposed to public policy, unless extortionate or unconscionable. |
| 50 | **Bowmakers Ltd v. Barnet Instruments Ltd** [1945] KB 65 | Property Recovery | Can a party recover property delivered under an illegal contract? | **Yes**, provided the claimant does not need to rely on the illegal contract to establish their right to ownership. |
| 51 | **Singh v. Ali** [1960] AC 167 | In Pari Delicto | Does the court assist a party who enters into an illegal vehicle transfer? | **No.** If both parties are equally at fault (*in pari delicto*), the court will not assist the claimant. |
| 52 | **ONGC Ltd. v. Western Geco International Ltd.** (2014) 9 SCC 263 | Public Policy Scope | What constitutes the "public policy of India" in commercial arbitrations? | It includes fundamental principles of Indian law, justice, morality, and adherence to the rule of audi alteram partem. |

## 🛡️ Category 6: Void Restraints — Non-Compete & Jurisdiction (Cases 53–65)

| # | Case Name & Citation | Sub-Category | Key Legal Issue | Core Judicial Holding (Precedent Ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 53 | **Niranjan Shankar Golikari v. Century Spinning & Mfg Co.** AIR 1967 SC 1098 | Employment Restraint | Is a non-compete clause applicable during the term of employment valid? | **Yes.** Restraints operating **during the term of employment** are valid and enforceable if reasonable; S. 27 only voids post-termination restraints. |
| 54 | **Superintendence Company of India v. Krishan Murgai** AIR 1980 SC 1717 | Post-Employment Restraint | Is a post-employment non-compete covenant void under S. 27? | **Yes.** All post-employment non-compete covenants are **void ab initio** in India. The "reasonableness" exception does not apply. |
| 55 | **Percept D'Mark (India) v. Zaheer Khan** AIR 2006 SC 1426 | Right of First Refusal | Does a right of first refusal post-contract constitute a restraint of trade? | **Yes.** Any clause extending constraints beyond the contract term (including restricting negotiations) violates S. 27. |
| 56 | **Diljeet Titus, Advocate v. Alfred A. Adebare & Ors.** 2006 (32) PTC 609 (Del) *(corrected — replaces a duplicate listing of Case #53; this is a distinct, real Delhi HC case)* | Trade Secrets Exception | Can a former employer restrain a departed employee/associate from using confidential work product? | **Yes.** Documents, drafts, and case files created in the course of employment belong to the employer; a departing employee cannot walk away with and use confidential material, even absent a non-compete clause. |
| 57 | **Madhub Chunder Poramanic v. Rajcoomar Doss** (1874) 14 Beng LR 76 | Restraint of Trade | Does Section 27 apply to partial restraints of trade? | **Yes.** Section 27 invalidates both partial and total restraints of trade; there is no scope for a common-law "reasonableness" test. |
| 58 | **Gujarat Bottling Co. Ltd. v. Coca-Cola Co.** AIR 1995 SC 2631 | Exclusive Distribution | Is an exclusive distribution agreement void under Section 27? | **No.** Exclusive dealing agreements operating **during the subsistence of the contract** promote trade and are valid. |
| 59 | **Modi Rubber Ltd. v. Alok Kumar** (1993) 2 LLJ 1122 | Training Bonds | Is a service bond requiring an employee to serve for 3 years after training valid? | **Yes.** Training bonds are valid if the employer incurs actual expenditure and the compensation represents a genuine pre-estimate of loss. |
| 60 | **Wipro Ltd. v. Beckman Coulter International** 2006 (3) ARBLR 118 | Non-Solicitation | Is a non-solicitation clause targeting employees void under Section 27? | **No.** A clause restraining the solicitation of active employees is valid and does not prevent them from practicing their trade. |
| 61 | **Hulas Metal Crafts v. Bank of Baroda** AIR 1987 SC 2341 | Exclusive Jurisdiction | Is a clause restricting jurisdiction to one of two competent courts valid? | **Yes.** If two courts have jurisdiction, an agreement limiting disputes to one of them is valid and does not violate S. 28. |
| 62 | **A.B.C. Laminart Pvt. Ltd. v. A.P. Agencies** AIR 1989 SC 1239 | Ouster of Jurisdiction | Can a contract create jurisdiction in a court that has none? | **No.** Parties cannot confer jurisdiction on a court that has no territorial or pecuniary jurisdiction under S. 28. |
| 63 | **Taff Vale Railway Co. v. Amalgamated Society of Railway Servants** [1901] AC 426 *(corrected — replaces fabricated "T.G. Colligan v. Glasgow, (1901) ILR 28 Cal 34," which does not exist in any traceable record; this is very likely what the entry was garbling)* | Trade Union Liability | Can a trade union be sued and held financially liable for losses caused by a strike? | **Yes.** A registered trade union can be sued in its own name and its funds held liable for tortious acts (such as inducing breach of contract) committed during a strike. |
| 64 | **Nordenfelt v. Maxim Nordenfelt** [1894] AC 535 | Goodwill Exception | Is a worldwide non-compete valid on the sale of a global armaments business? | **Yes.** Under the goodwill exception, a global restraint is valid if it is reasonable in the interest of the parties and the public. |
| 65 | **Durgadevi v. Mathuradas** AIR 1924 Nag 232 | Professional Practice | Is an agreement restricting a lawyer's right to practice void? | **Yes.** Any agreement restraining a professional from practicing their profession violates Section 27. |

## 🌋 Category 7: Force Majeure & Frustration of Contract (Cases 66–75)

| # | Case Name & Citation | Sub-Category | Key Legal Issue | Core Judicial Holding (Precedent Ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 66 | **Satyabrata Ghose v. Mugneeram Bangur & Co.** AIR 1954 SC 44 | Frustration | Does temporary requisition of land by government frustrate a development contract? | **No.** Frustration requires the destruction of the contract's fundamental basis, not a temporary delay or commercial difficulty. |
| 67 | **Energy Watchdog v. CERC** (2017) 14 SCC 80 | Force Majeure Clause | Does an increase in fuel cost constitute a force majeure event? | **No.** Commercial hardship or tariff increases do not frustrate a contract. An express clause is governed by Section 32, not Section 56. |
| 68 | **Sushila Devi v. Hari Singh** AIR 1971 SC 1756 | Impossibility | Does partition of India frustrate a lease agreement for land in Pakistan? | **Yes.** Self-induced frustration is invalid, but partition made performance physically and legally impossible, invoking S. 56. |
| 69 | **Krell v. Henry** [1903] 2 KB 740 | Foundation Destruction | Is a room rental contract frustrated if the coronation procession is cancelled? | **Yes.** The procession was the sole foundation of the contract. Its cancellation discharged the parties. |
| 70 | **Taylor v. Caldwell** (1863) 3 B&S 826 | Subject Matter Loss | Is a music hall lease frustrated if the hall burns down? | **Yes.** If the existence of a specific subject matter is essential to performance, its destruction excuses both parties. |
| 71 | **Tsakiroglou & Co Ltd v. Noblee Thorl GmbH** [1962] AC 93 | Alternative Routes | Is a shipping contract frustrated by closure of the Suez Canal? | **No.** A contract is not frustrated if an alternative route (e.g. Cape of Good Hope) exists, even if it is longer and more costly. |
| 72 | **Metropolitan Water Board v. Dick Kerr & Co.** [1918] AC 119 | Government Intervention | Does a wartime government order to halt construction frustrate the contract? | **Yes.** The order created an indefinite delay that fundamentally changed the character of the performance. |
| 73 | **Ganga Saran v. Ram Charan** AIR 1952 SC 9 | Supply Shortage | Does a mill worker strike frustrate a seller's obligation to deliver goods? | **No.** A strike does not make performance impossible if the seller could have procured goods from the open market. |
| 74 | **Paradine v. Jane** (1647) Aleyn 26 | Absolute Covenants | Is a tenant liable for rent if evicted by an invading army? | **Yes.** A party who binds themselves by an absolute contract must perform it, notwithstanding accidents or external forces. |
| 75 | **Alopi Parshad & Sons v. Union of India** AIR 1960 SC 578 | Commercial Hardship | Can a party claim higher rates due to an unexpected rise in expenses? | **No.** Contracts are not frustrated merely because performance becomes more expensive or less profitable than expected. |

## 💥 Category 8: Breach, Damages, and Forfeiture (Cases 76–88)

| # | Case Name & Citation | Sub-Category | Key Legal Issue | Core Judicial Holding (Precedent Ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 76 | **Hadley v. Baxendale** (1854) 9 Exch 341 | Remoteness | Is a carrier liable for lost profits if a replacement mill shaft is delayed? | **No.** Damages are limited to losses arising naturally, or those in the contemplation of both parties when contracting. |
| 77 | **Kailash Nath Associates v. Delhi Development Authority** (2015) 4 SCC 136 | Liquidated Damages | Can earnest money be forfeited without proof of actual damage? | **No.** S. 74 applies to all penalty/forfeiture clauses. Reasonable compensation is awarded only if **actual damage or loss** is established. |
| 78 | **ONGC Ltd v. Saw Pipes Ltd** AIR 2003 SC 2629 | Agreed Damages | Can a buyer recover liquidated damages without proving actual loss? | **Yes**, if the court is satisfied that the agreed sum is a genuine pre-estimate of damage and actual loss is difficult to prove. |
| 79 | **Maula Bux v. Union of India** AIR 1970 SC 1955 | Earnest Money | Does Section 74 apply to forfeiture of earnest money deposits? | **Yes.** Any clause prescribing forfeiture of deposits upon breach is subject to S. 74 and requires proof of loss. |
| 80 | **Fateh Chand v. Balkishan Dass** AIR 1963 SC 1405 | Penalty Clauses | Can a seller retain all paid installments upon buyer default? | **No.** Section 74 overrides penal clauses. The court will only award reasonable compensation based on actual loss. |
| 81 | **Dunlop Pneumatic Tyre v. New Garage** [1915] AC 79 | Penalty vs Pre-estimate | How do you distinguish between a penalty and a genuine pre-estimate? | A sum is a penalty if it is **extravagant and unconscionable** in comparison with the greatest conceivable loss. |
| 82 | **Victoria Laundry Ltd v. Newman Industries** [1949] 2 KB 528 | Loss of Profits | Can a laundry recover profits from lost lucrative dyeing contracts? | Only if the defendant was **made aware of the special opportunity** at the time the contract was executed. |
| 83 | **Payzu v. Saunders** [1919] 2 KB 581 | Mitigation of Loss | Is a buyer required to accept a seller's offer of cash terms to mitigate loss? | **Yes.** Aggrieved parties must take all reasonable steps to mitigate their losses and cannot recover damages for avoidable loss. |
| 84 | **Frost v. Knight** (1872) LR 7 Ex 111 | Anticipatory Breach | Can a party sue immediately upon an anticipatory breach? | **Yes.** If a party repudiates their obligation before the performance date, the promisee can sue for damages immediately. |
| 85 | **Hochster v. De La Tour** (1853) 2 E&B 678 | Immediate Suit | Does a courier have to wait for the contract date to sue for breach? | **No.** An anticipatory breach allows the injured party to treat the contract as ended and bring an action immediately. |
| 86 | **Simplex Concrete Piles (India) Ltd. v. Union of India** ILR (2010) II Delhi 699 / 2010 (115) DRJ 616 | Barring Damages | Is a contractual clause completely barring a contractor from claiming damages for delay valid? | **No.** Contractual clauses that completely disentitle a party from claiming statutory damages for breach are void under Section 23 of the Contract Act as contrary to public policy. |
| 87 | **A.K.AS. Jamal v. Moolla Dawood** (1916) 43 IA 6 | Date of Breach | How is the market value of shares calculated for breach of contract? | Damages for non-acceptance of shares are calculated based on the market price on the **exact date of the breach**. |
| 88 | **Karsandas H. Thacker v. Saran Engineering Co. Ltd.** AIR 1965 SC 1981 / (1965) 2 SCR 214 | Indirect Damages | Are indirect or remote losses recoverable under Section 73 if the other party was not notified of special circumstances? | **No.** Section 73 excludes remote and indirect losses. A party cannot recover special damages (like liabilities to third parties) unless the other party had notice of the special contract at execution. |

## 🏢 Category 9: MSMED Act 2006 Overrides (Cases 89–94)

| # | Case Name & Citation | Sub-Category | Key Legal Issue | Core Judicial Holding (Precedent Ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 89 | **Gujarat State Civil Supplies Corp v. Mahakali Foods** 2022 SCC OnLine SC 1492 | MSME Override | Does the MSMED Act override independent arbitration agreements? | **Yes.** The MSMED Act is a special legislation. The MSEFC dispute resolution mechanism under S. 18 overrides any private agreement, even where an independent arbitration clause exists. |
| 90 | **Silpi Industries v. Kerala State Road Transport Corp** 2021 INSC 314 | Non-Obstante | Do Sections 15–23 of the MSMED Act override inconsistent laws? | **Yes.** Section 24 gives Sections 15–23 overriding effect over any other inconsistent law for the time being in force. |
| 91 | **Principal Chief Engineer v. M/s. Manibhai and Bros** AIR 2016 Guj 123 / (2017) 205 Comp Cas 351 (SC) | Facilitation Council | Can a party bypass the MSEFC Facilitation Council by invoking a private arbitration clause or filing a civil suit? | **No.** The MSMED Act is a special law with overriding effect. Section 18 statutory dispute resolution (conciliation/arbitration) overrides any private contract or arbitration clause. |
| 92 | **Modern Industries v. Steel Authority of India** (2010) 5 SCC 44 | Interest Penalties | Is interest payable to an MSME buyer subject to Section 74 limits? | **No.** Statutory interest liability on delayed payments under the predecessor 1993 Act (and, by extension, the MSMED Act's S. 16) is a fixed statutory entitlement, not subject to modification via the Facilitation Council overreaching its scope. |
| 93 | **Jharkhand Urja Vikas Nigam Ltd. v. State of Rajasthan** (2021) 20 SCC 665 / 2021 INSC 812 | MSEFC Orders | Can the MSEFC issue an order directing payment without completing the statutory conciliation and arbitration process? | **No.** The Council must follow the mandatory sequence under S. 18: first attempt conciliation, and if it fails, initiate separate arbitration proceedings. It cannot club the two or issue an order without arbitration. |
| 94 | **Secur Industries Ltd. v. Godrej & Boyce Mfg. Co.** (2004) INSC 127 | Pending Reference | Does a reference to the MSEFC stay pending civil proceedings? | **Yes.** Once a reference is validly made, a civil/High Court has no jurisdiction to stay the Facilitation Council's proceedings. |

## 🔒 Category 10: Data Privacy & DPDP Fundamental Precedents (Cases 95–100)

| # | Case Name & Citation | Sub-Category | Key Legal Issue | Core Judicial Holding (Precedent Ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 95 | **Justice K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India** (2017) 10 SCC 1 | Privacy Right | Is privacy a constitutionally protected right in India? | **Yes.** The right to privacy is a fundamental right guaranteed under Article 21 of the Constitution of India. |
| 96 | **WhatsApp LLC v. Competition Commission of India** 2022 SCC OnLine Del 2582 *(corrected — was misnamed "v.
