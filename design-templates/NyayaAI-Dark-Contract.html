Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

TechNova Solutions Pvt. Ltd. × Vertex Analytics LLP

Effective 15.11.2024 · Ref TN-VA-NDA-2024-0892 · 3-year term

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is made and entered into on this 15th day of November, 2024 ("Effective Date") by and between:

TechNova Solutions Private Limited, a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, having CIN U72200KA2019PTC128847, with its registered office at Prestige Tech Park, Marathahalli, Bengaluru, Karnataka 560103 (hereinafter referred to as the "Disclosing Party");

— AND —

Vertex Analytics LLP, a limited liability partnership registered under the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008, having LLIN AAB-1234, with its principal place of business at Cyber Hub, DLF Phase III, Gurugram, Haryana 122002 (hereinafter referred to as the "Receiving Party").

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to explore a potential business relationship (the "Purpose") and may disclose certain confidential and proprietary information to each other in connection therewith. NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the Parties agree as follows:

§ 1 Definitions

Safe

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other, whether in oral, written, electronic, or other form, including but not limited to trade secrets, business plans, customer data, technical specifications, source code, financial information, and marketing strategies, regardless of whether marked as confidential.

Standard definition scope. Carve-outs for independently developed information and forced disclosures properly included.

§ 2 Confidentiality Obligations

Safe

2.1 The Receiving Party shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not disclose such information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, except to its employees and advisors on a need-to-know basis.

2.2 The Receiving Party shall use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and shall exercise at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar importance, but no less than reasonable care.

§ 2.3 Non-Compete Restriction

Void · ICA §27

The Receiving Party covenants that, for a period of five (5) years commencing from the Effective Date or termination of this Agreement, it shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in, own, manage, operate, control, be employed by, or have any financial interest in any business that competes with the Disclosing Party within the Republic of India, irrespective of the nature of such engagement.

Statutory conflict: Indian Contract Act, 1872 — §27 declares agreements in restraint of trade void, except sale of goodwill. 5-year restriction is patently unreasonable. Niranjan Shankar Golikari v. Century Spinning (1967) permits only reasonable restrictions during employment term.

§ 3 Term and Termination

Safe

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and remain in force for a period of three (3) years. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days prior written notice. The confidentiality obligations herein shall survive termination for a further period of three (3) years.

§ 4.1 Payment Terms

Review · MSME

All invoices submitted pursuant to any engagement arising from this Agreement shall be payable by the Receiving Party within ninety (90) days from the date of receipt of a valid invoice. Disputed amounts shall be paid within fifteen (15) days of resolution.

Compliance issue: MSME Development Act, 2006 — §15 mandates payment to MSME suppliers within 45 days. The 90-day term attracts daily compounded interest under §16 and prohibits recovery under §17.

§ 5 Intellectual Property

Safe

All Confidential Information remains the sole property of the Disclosing Party. No intellectual property rights are transferred or licensed under this Agreement. The Receiving Party shall promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any actual or suspected misuse of such Confidential Information.

§ 6 Indemnification

Review

Each Party (the "Indemnifying Party") shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other Party from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or in connection with any breach of this Agreement, without limitation as to amount.

Uncapped indemnity exposure. Consider reciprocal cap at fees paid in preceding 12 months and exclude indirect/consequential losses.

§ 7 Dispute Resolution

Unenforceable

Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Singapore, and the Parties hereby submit to such jurisdiction. The language of any proceedings shall be English.

Enforceability issue: Exclusive foreign jurisdiction clause is unenforceable where parties have no real connection to Singapore. Indo Air SA v. Morgan Electric & Motor Co. (1995) — Indian courts retain jurisdiction under §20 CPC if cause of action arises in India. Suggested amendment: exclusive jurisdiction of competent courts at Bengaluru.

§ 8 Governing Law

Safe

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Republic of India, without giving effect to any conflict of law principles.

§ 9 Limitation of Liability

Review

In no event shall either Party be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages. The aggregate liability of either Party under this Agreement shall not exceed the amounts paid by the Receiving Party to the Disclosing Party in the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to liability.

Standard market cap. Verify against contract value — for high-value engagements, consider negotiating higher cap or specific carve-outs for IP indemnity.
