Opinion on Post-Termination Non-Compete Restraints

A

Executive Summary

Post-termination non-compete clauses in employment agreements are generally void under Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872. The courts consistently distinguish between restraints during the term of employment (which are valid) and post-termination restraints (which are void). The only exception is the sale of goodwill.

B

Statutory Analysis

The primary statutory hurdle is Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, which expressly declares agreements in restraint of trade as void. Unlike jurisdictions that apply a "reasonableness" test, Indian courts strictly adhere to the statutory language.

However, courts have interpreted this to mean that negative covenants during the term of employment are valid, as they do not operate as a restraint of trade but rather protect the employer's legitimate business interests. The distinction lies in whether the restraint operates during or after the termination of the contract.

Furthermore, if the agreement involves handling sensitive data, Section 72A of the IT Act, 2000 provides penal consequences for disclosure, but does not validate a broader non-compete restraint.
